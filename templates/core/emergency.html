Feature 4 — Emergency

Emergencies and SOS

If you are in immediate danger, get to safety first. This page lists public emergency lines. One-tap SOS recording is not part of the current web MVP—the product today is this site in your browser.

Public helplines (verify locally)

Numbers can change. Confirm with official government or police sources for your state.

  • 112 — National emergency number (many states)
  • 199 — Police emergency (widely publicised)
  • State commands — Your state police public relations office can give current hotlines.
  • NHRC — National Human Rights Commission for complaints about rights violations (see official site for contacts).

SOS recording

A future version might add one-tap audio or video recording during encounters, with secure backup and share to a trusted contact or lawyer. That needs device permissions beyond what a normal website can do. We are not building that in the MVP; if we add it later, it would likely be a separate native app or a heavily permissioned installable experience—not a promise for now.

Safety first

  • • No recording is worth physical harm. Comply where you must, then seek help.
  • • The Law does not provide real-time monitoring or emergency dispatch.
