Feature 7 — Quick cards

What to say right now

Short scripts you can use under pressure. They are educational prompts, not legal advice. Stay calm, do not escalate, and seek a lawyer or trusted help when you can.

Police stop, search, or demand for money

You are stopped in public; officers ask for a bribe or threaten you.

“I am willing to cooperate. I prefer to handle any fine through the proper process. Please tell me the offence you are charging me with and the station or court where this will be addressed. I will not pay cash on the roadside.”

If you fear for your safety, prioritise getting to a safe place. You can use emergency numbers and document what happened afterwards in chat or your case log.

Asked to come to the station or detained

You are told you are under arrest or must follow officers.

“Please tell me why I am being detained and who is in charge here. I want to contact someone I trust and, when appropriate, a lawyer. I will not resist, but I need to understand the reason and my rights in this process.”

Laws on arrest and detention exist to protect you; specifics depend on the situation. Use chat for structured follow-up—especially if you were held without clear reason.

Eviction notice or lockout threat

A landlord or agent pressures you to leave without following lawful process.

“I need any eviction step in writing, with the proper notice period under our agreement and the law. I will not accept a forced lockout without due process. I am willing to discuss payment or a lawful timeline through the right channel.”

Tenancy rules vary; wrongful eviction is a serious matter. Save messages, notices, and photos, and ask for guidance in chat.

Workplace: unlawful levy or threat

Someone in authority demands informal payment or threatens your job without a clear basis.

“I want to resolve this properly. Please put the request or accusation in writing so I can understand it and respond. I am ready to follow lawful procedures and speak through the right office or union representative.”

Document dates, names, and witnesses. Labour and regulatory routes depend on your sector—use chat to narrow the issue.
