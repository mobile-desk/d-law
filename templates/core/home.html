What we offer

Legal help should not feel out of reach. The Law is a guided front door—structured, Nigeria-aware information first, then optional connection to people who can take your case further.

Rights guidance

Clear questions and answers about what happened, where, and who was involved—so you see your situation in context, not a cold checklist.

Anonymous first

Explore without signing up. Share only what feels safe. Create an account later to save your case and message a matched professional.

Lawyers & activists

Not every issue belongs in court on day one. We include NGOs and defenders where that fit matters—not only barristers.

WhatsApp & SMS

Chat with the same guided assistant where you already spend time—WhatsApp and SMS—so you can get clarity without opening a browser.

How it works

  1. 1
    Guided chat

    Describe what happened on the site, on WhatsApp, or by SMS. The assistant follows up with structured questions and plain-language education.

  2. 2
    Clarify your issue

    We help surface the type of matter—civil, criminal concern, police conduct, safety, or human rights. You can edit before sharing.

  3. 3
    Optional match

    Request a verified professional whose focus fits your category, when one is available.

  4. 4
    One place to follow up

    With an account, track status, message your professional, and get alerts—without scattered threads.

Who this is for

Residents & workers

When stigma or safety has stopped you from asking for help, anonymous chat lowers the barrier. You decide when to identify yourself.

Important

The Law provides legal information and connection services, not legal advice. Nothing here creates a lawyer–client relationship. For urgent danger, contact emergency services or a trusted helpline. Confirm strategy with a qualified professional before acting or refraining from legal action.
